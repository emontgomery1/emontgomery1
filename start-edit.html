Important Information

By installing and implementing the Jornaya campaign script, you acknowledge and grant permission for Jornaya to provide Visual Playback of lead events captured on your website(s) to mutual clients that audit your LeadiD tokens, upon written request from a mutual client to Jornaya. If applicable, you are solely responsible for securing prior written permissions from any company you may work with who is the original creator of the lead; and your grant of permission hereunder constitutes such party’s or parties’ permission(s) as well. We will provide you written notice of requests for Visual Playback for leads with a LeadiD token which you provide to a mutual client. Visual Playback provides a virtual rendering of the actual event data captured by Jornaya at the time of the original lead event, for the purpose of providing facts about the content that was or was not rendered on the web page (for example, a visual rendering of a disclosure or other content), as well as the consumer actions exhibited during the lead event (for example, what type of consent was obtained, if any). The use of Visual Playback is subject to our Privacy Policy posted on our Site.

Step 1: Database

Create an additional field in your database to store a LeadiD token with each lead that you generate. LeadiD tokens are in the form of a globally unique identifier with 36 total characters.

A LeadiD will look like this:
4xyz78b9-0cdc-43a7-98ea-2b680a5313a2 (example only)

Step 2: Form

Add a new hidden input field to each of your lead generation forms with “leadid_token” as the id attribute. This hidden field must have the id attribute: “leadid_token.” Jornaya will populate this new hidden field in real time with a unique LeadiD token each time your form renders.

The form field should look like this:
<form> ... <inputid="leadid_token"name="universal_leadid"type="hidden"value=""/> ... </form>

Step 3: Reset Script

The script should be placed after the </form > tag and immediately before the end of the </body > tag. If your forms have multiple steps/ follow a funnel, then this script should be placed on every page in the funnel, including the 'Thank You' page, if one exits.

Code:
</form> ... < script id= "LeadiDscript" type="text/javascript">// <!--(function(){var s= document.createElement('script');s.id = 'LeadiDscript_campaign'; s.type = 'text/javascript':s.async = true; s.src = '//{your-code-snippet-here}.js? snippet_version=2'; var LeadiDscript = document.getElementById('LeadiDscript'); LeadiDscript.parentNode.insertBefore(s, LeadiDscript);})();// --> </script><noscript><img src='//{your-code-snippet-here}&snippet_version=2'/></noscript> (example only, do not copy) ... </body>

Step 4: Test and Post

Contact Jornaya to certify your implementation. Refer to the Getting Started Guide of your lead buyer(s) for specific instructions to update your post to add the LeadiD field. You will continue to deliver your leads directly to your buyers as you currently do.

FAQs

What is a Universal LeadiD, LeadiD or LeadiD token?

A Universal LeadiD, LeadiD or LeadiD token is a 36 hexadecimal character identifier with the format XXXXXXXX-XXXX-XXXX-XXXX-XXXXXXXXXXXX (also known as a "GUID" or globally unique identifier).

It is generated when a webpage loads containing the Jornaya campaign javascript snippet, and allows us to uniquely identify a lead event.  

Jornaya's standard implementation stores the token in the hidden input field id="leadid_token" and will persist through each page where the same Jornaya script has been implemented. This ensures we capture and link the entire lead event to the same LeadiD token.

Since it is unique to a specific lead event, only one token should be generated when the page is loaded.

What does the Jornaya Campaign script do?

The Jornaya campaign script is designed to work asynchronously in the background and not interfere with the user experience. When the page loads and the script fires, a LeadiD Token is generated.
